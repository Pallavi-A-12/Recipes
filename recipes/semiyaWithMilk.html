Semiya Payasam

INGREDIENTS

  • Semiya (Vermicelli): 1 cup (broken into smaller pieces)
  • Whole Milk: 1 litre (4 cups)
  • Sugar: ½ cup (adjust to taste)
  • Ghee: 2 tablespoons
  • Cashew Nuts & Raisins: 2 tablespoons each
  • Cardamom Powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Saffron Strands: 7 to 8 strands (optional, soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)

STEP BY STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Fry the Nuts: Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium-low flame. Roast the cashews until golden, then add the raisins until they plump up. Remove and set them aside.
  2. Roast the Semiya: Add another tablespoon of ghee to the same pan. Add the semiya and stir continuously on low heat for 2–3 minutes until it turns a light golden-brown colour. Skip this if using pre-roasted semiya, though a light toss in ghee still enhances flavor.
  3. Boil the Milk: Pour the whole milk carefully into the pan with the roasted semiya. Bring the mixture to a rolling boil on medium heat, stirring frequently to ensure it does not stick to the bottom.
  4. Simmer and Cook: Turn the flame down to low-medium. Let the semiya simmer in the milk for about 8–10 minutes until the strands become soft and cooked through. The milk will naturally begin to thicken and turn creamy.
  5. Sweeten and Flavour: Add the sugar and stir well until completely dissolved. Add the cardamom powder and the soaked saffron milk. Simmer for an additional 3–4 minutes.
  6. Garnish and Serve: Turn off the heat. Stir in the fried cashews and raisins. Serve the dish warm, or place it in the refrigerator to serve as a chilled dessert.

Note

Semiya continues to absorb liquid and will thicken significantly as it cools. If it becomes too thick, simply stir in a few tablespoons of warm milk before serving.
