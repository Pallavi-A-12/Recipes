Gutti Vankaya

INTEGREDIENTS

  • Brinjals: 8-10 small, tender purple or green brinjals.

    • Stuffing Masala:

    • Peanuts: 1/2 cup (dry roasted).
    • Sesame Seeds: 2 tbsp.
    • Desiccated Coconut: 2-3 tbsp.
    • Spices: 1 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 3-4 cloves, 1 inch cinnamon, 2 cardamom pods, and 4-6 dry red chillies.
    • Aromatics: 1 inch ginger, 3-4 garlic cloves, and 1 small onion (roughly chopped).
    • Tang & Sweet: 1 tbsp tamarind pulp, 1 tsp jaggery (optional), salt, and 1/2 tsp turmeric powder.

    For Tempering & Gravy:

  • 3-4 tbsp oil, 1 tsp mustard seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, a few curry leaves, and fresh coriander for garnish.

STEP BY STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Prepare the Masala Paste:
    • Dry roast peanuts, sesame seeds, coriander seeds, cumin, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, and red chillies separately until aromatic.
    • Add desiccated coconut at the end and toast briefly until golden.
    • Cool the roasted spices and grind them into a smooth paste along with onion, ginger, garlic, tamarind, salt, and jaggery, using a little water if needed.
  2. Prep and Stuff Brinjals:
    • Wash the brinjals. Make a cross-shaped (+) slit at the bottom, keeping the stalk intact.
    • Soak them in salted water for 10-15 minutes to prevent browning and remove bitterness.
    • Stuff the prepared masala paste generously into the slits of each brinjal. Keep any leftover paste aside.
  3. Cook the Curry:
    • Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan or kadai. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and curry leaves.
    • Carefully arrange the stuffed brinjals in the pan. Cover and cook on low-medium flame for 5-8 minutes, turning them occasionally until the skins wilt slightly.
    • Add any leftover masala paste, 1 cup of water, and more tamarind extract if desired.
    • Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes on low heat until the brinjals are completely soft and oil begins to separate/ooze out from the edges.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
