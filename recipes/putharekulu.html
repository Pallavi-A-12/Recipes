Putharukulu

INGREDIENTS

    For the Sheets (Rice Paper):

  • Raw Rice: 1 cup (preferably Jaya biyyam or aged rice)
  • Water: As needed to create a very thin, watery batter

    • For the Filling:

  • Ghee: ½ cup, melted
  • Sweetener: ½ cup powdered sugar or grated jaggery
  • Nuts: ¼ cup finely chopped cashews, almonds, or pistachios
  • Cardamom Powder: 1 teaspoon for aroma

STEP BY STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Prepare the Rice Batter
    • Soak: Wash and soak the raw rice for at least 2 to 6 hours.
    • Grind: Grind the rice into a very smooth, fine paste using a wet grinder or blender, adding water gradually.
    • Dilute: The final batter should be very thin and watery (running consistency). If it’s too thick, the sheets won't be translucent.
  2. Make the Rice Sheets
    • Heat the Pan: Heat a flat-surfaced tawa or non-stick pan over medium heat.
    • Apply Batter: Dip a thin cotton cloth (like a clean handkerchief) into the batter. Swiftly spread or "dab" the wet cloth across the hot surface to leave a thin film of batter.
    • Cook & Peel: Within seconds, the layer will turn crisp and start to lift at the edges. Gently peel it off and set it aside. Repeat to make several sheets.
  3. Assemble the Sweet
    • Soften: Take 2–3 rice sheets. Because they are brittle, dab them lightly with a slightly wet cloth to make them pliable for folding.
  4. Layer Filling:
    • Brush a sheet generously with melted ghee.
    • Sprinkle the powdered sugar (or jaggery), chopped nuts, and cardamom powder evenly over it.
    • Place another sheet on top and repeat the ghee and filling layers.
  5. Fold & Roll:
    • Fold the sides inward and roll the stacked sheets into a tight, cylindrical "paper" roll.

Tips for Success

Batter Consistency:

If your sheets are too thick, add more water to the batter until it is almost like water.

Filling Varieties:

While sugar is common, the Bellam (Jaggery) version is highly prized for its deep, traditional flavor.

Storage:

Once prepared, these can last for up to 15–60 days in an airtight container, though they are best eaten fresh for maximum crunch.
